Multiple Art Days

Le salon des pratiques éditoriales dans l'art contemporain aujourd'hui

22, 23 et 24
mai 2015

11:00 - 19:00
vendredi en nocture

La Maison Rouge,
10 Boulevard de la Bastille,
75012 Paris

Nouveau rendez-vous annuel dédié à toute forme d’objets d’art multipliés, le MAD fait acte et offre pendant trois jours un panorama des pratiques éditoriales d’aujourd’hui : de l’édition fanzine aux œuvres rares: prints, multiples, livres, films et disques d’artistes...

Investissant l’ensemble des espaces d’un des lieux emblématiques des collectionneurs et amateur d'art contemporain, MAD réunira une centaine de producteurs internationaux qui ont pour point commun la qualité curatoriale de leurs propositions.

Bien plus qu’un salon, MAD est une exposition des projets de chacun des participants.

Ces journées seront accompagnées d’une série de rencontres, performances artistiques et débats sur la pratique éditoriale dans l’art contemporain avec les artistes, graphistes, responsables de collections publiques, éditeurs, collectionneurs, chercheurs et libraires venus du monde entier.

Au plaisir de vous recevoir au MAD !

Multiple Art Days

A NEW FAIR DEVOTED TO CONTEMPORARY ART EDITIONS IN ALL ITS FORMS

La Maison Rouge,
10 Boulevard de la Bastille,
75012 Paris

MAD is a new fair devoted to artistic multiples of all kinds. Hosted annually, the event will encompass a wide spectrum of contemporary publishing and art editions from fanzines to rare, limited-edition originals: prints, multiples, artists’ books, photographs, videos and records.

The event will be hosted by the Maison Rouge, Antoine de Galbert Foundation, a Parisian location emblematic of contemporary creation, which alternates thematic exhibitions with works from private art collections. More than 100 international participants (galleries, international and local publishers, large and small presses artists, institutions) will show their editions in the foundation’s 1,300 square meters of exhibition space.

Much more than a fair, MAD is also an exhibition. Each exhibitor will focus on presenting only one or two projects. Breaking with the habitual fair esthetic of the booth, the space will remain open and without partitions, encouraging the public to move freely through the space, stimulating dialogue between participants and visitors, and enhancing the works on display.

In addition, MAD will also feature furniture by French artist Stéphane Magnin from his “Do It Yourself” library. Exhibitors will be invited to choose from a line of his “open source” furniture in cardboard and wood (tables, chairs, sofas, bookshelves...).

During these three days, a program of conferences, panel discussions, performances and debates will bring together artists, designers, curators, publishers, researchers and book dealers from around the world.
